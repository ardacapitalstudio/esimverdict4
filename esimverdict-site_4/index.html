How to choose

  • Trip length. Under a week: a small fixed plan. A month or more: regional plans or unlimited.
  • Data appetite. Maps and messages: 1-3GB. Streaming and hotspotting: think Holafly or Holiday.com unlimited.
  • Speed caps. "Unlimited" rarely means uncapped. Check the daily fast-data allowance - typically 2-3GB/day before throttling.
  • Device check. iPhone XS or newer, Pixel 3 or newer, Samsung S20 or newer all support eSIM.

Install in 5 steps

  1. 1

    Buy your plan and receive a QR code by email.

  2. 2

    Settings → Mobile Data → Add eSIM (iPhone), or Settings → Connections → SIM Manager (Android).

  3. 3

    Scan the QR code with your camera.

  4. 4

    Follow the prompts to activate.

  5. 5

    Land, select the eSIM as your data line, and you're online.

⚠ Install before you fly. Some plans need wifi to activate.

FAQ?

Big questions

What is the best eSIM for travel? →
Our top pick is Airalo: 200+ destinations, a polished 4.7-star app and fair pricing from $4.00 per GB. Holafly leads for unlimited data, while Mobimatter's marketplace has the outright cheapest plans from $3.49.
How much does a travel eSIM cost? →
Fixed-data plans start around $3.49-4.50 for 1GB (Mobimatter, Holiday.com, Nomad, Airalo). Unlimited plans start at about $6.90 per day with Holafly, with monthly global options around $65; Airalo, Nomad and Holiday.com offer unlimited tiers too. Either way it is typically far cheaper than roaming charges.
Does my phone support eSIM? →
Most phones from 2018 onwards do: iPhone XS and newer, Google Pixel 3 and newer, Samsung Galaxy S20 and newer. Check Settings for an "Add eSIM" or "SIM Manager" option to be sure.
Can I keep my normal SIM at the same time? →
Yes. Dual SIM means your physical SIM stays active for calls and texts while the eSIM handles data. Just turn off data roaming on your home SIM so you don't get billed twice.
What happens if I run out of data? →
Most providers (Airalo, Nomad, Holiday.com, Mobimatter) let you top up in the app in seconds, though Nomad and Mobimatter top-ups depend on the plan. Holafly is unlimited so it never runs out; unlimited tiers elsewhere slow down after a daily fast-data allowance rather than stopping.
