Yesim eSIM Review 2026: Flexible Swiss All-Rounder With Small Print Worth Reading

Yesim eSIM review for 2026: real prices, Pay & Fly rates, unlimited fair-use limits, virtual numbers, refunds and complaints. A balanced independent verdict.

By the eSIM Verdict research desk Last checked 19 July 2026 13 min read

Yesim is a Swiss travel eSIM provider, founded in 2019 and operated by Genesis Group AG from Baar in the canton of Zug, that claims more than four million users and coverage in over 200 destinations through partnerships with 800+ mobile operators. It sells the usual fixed-data packages and unlimited plans, but its real point of difference is flexibility: a Pay & Fly pay-as-you-go wallet that works in 170+ countries, one reusable eSIM profile, rentable virtual phone numbers and a free VPN on iOS.

Our research found a service that genuinely suits frequent, multi-country travellers who value one app for everything, with mid-range pricing that is rarely the cheapest option for any single trip. There are also caveats you should weigh before buying. Yesim does not clearly publish the high-speed allowance behind its "unlimited" plans, refunds under 10 euros are paid in app credit rather than cash, and customer reports suggest support can be far slower than the advertised average.

On our standard criteria - price, coverage, network quality, plan flexibility, transparency, ease of use, hotspot support and customer service - Yesim scores strongly on flexibility and ease of use, adequately on price and coverage, and poorly on transparency. This review sets out the evidence.

At a glance

  • Coverage: 200+ destinations claimed; independent testing counts roughly 147 countries with fixed plans and 170+ with pay-as-you-go access.
  • Plan types: single-country packages, 10 regional bundles, 6 global packages, unlimited daily-pass plans and the Pay & Fly per-MB wallet.
  • Prices: mid-range; independent reviewers quote about $4.50 for 1GB/7 days in popular European destinations and $12 for 5GB, $19 for 10GB and $24 for 20GB over 30 days in tested markets.
  • Unlimited plans: 7-day passes around $20.40 (Thailand) to $28.80 (USA, France); the daily high-speed allowance before throttling is not clearly published.
  • Pay & Fly: pay-as-you-go data in 170+ countries from roughly $1 per GB in the cheapest markets; wallet credit stays valid for 12 months.
  • Calls and SMS: data-only; separate virtual numbers (UK, US, Netherlands, Canada) from $3.60 per month for receiving SMS.
  • Hotspot: permitted on all plans at no extra cost.
  • Extras: free VPN for iOS users, Ycoins loyalty scheme returning 5% on purchases, plan sharing.
  • Refunds: 30-day window for unused, unexpired plans only; refunds under 10 euros paid as Ycoins app credit; reviews take up to 15 business days.
  • App ratings (19 July 2026): about 4.6 on the App Store, 4.4+ on Google Play.
  • Trustpilot: about 4.3, from a modest base of roughly 1,900 reviews.

Plans and pricing in detail

Fixed-data packages

Yesim's core catalogue is conventional: data packages for single countries, ten regional bundles and six global packages covering 140+ countries, with validity from one day through weekly, fortnightly and monthly options. Independent testing in 2026 found 5GB for $12, 10GB for $19 and 20GB for $24 over 30 days in tested European markets, and entry pricing of about $4.50 for 1GB over 7 days. One catch for light users: reviewers note that several destinations skip the small 1GB and 2GB tiers entirely, so the minimum spend can be higher than at rivals. New customers are courted with promotional offers such as 500MB for $0.54.

That positions Yesim as mid-market. Per-gigabyte costs of around $3 at higher tiers are reasonable, but Airalo, Nomad and MobiMatter frequently undercut it, and one 2026 test across eight countries priced Yesim 15-20% above Saily on comparable plans. If the lowest price per usable gigabyte is your only criterion, start with our best budget eSIM guide instead.

Unlimited plans: the transparency problem

Yesim sells unlimited plans as daily passes, with 7-day options at $20.40 for Thailand and $28.80 for the USA and France at the time of checking. Longer "Unlim" bundles run up to a year. The prices are competitive against Holafly's $6.90 per day benchmark once a trip passes four or five days.

The problem is disclosure. As one long-running reviewer put it after testing, Yesim "does not clearly disclose how much high-speed data is included" before the fair usage policy slows you down, and the company attributes throttling to the policies of local carriers. Airalo, by contrast, publishes a precise 3GB/day threshold and a 1 Mbps floor speed. Holafly publishes no daily threshold either, but has a longer track record of genuinely usable unlimited data, as our Airalo review and Holafly coverage discuss. Buying an unlimited plan without knowing the throttle point is a gamble we cannot score well on transparency.

Pay & Fly: the genuinely different bit

Pay & Fly is Yesim's pay-as-you-go mode: you load a wallet, keep one eSIM profile installed, and are billed per megabyte in 170+ countries at rates that start around a dollar per gigabyte in the cheapest markets and rise steeply in expensive ones. Wallet credit remains valid for 12 months, and the same eSIM reactivates on your next trip with no new installation.

For a certain traveller this is the best feature in the review. If you take short, frequent trips across many countries and mostly need maps, messaging and email, a topped-up wallet beats buying a fresh package every weekend. Check the per-country rate before relying on it anywhere unusual, because pay-as-you-go pricing varies enormously by destination. For itineraries crossing several borders on one trip, compare the regional bundle route in our best eSIM for multiple destinations guide.

Virtual numbers, VPN and the super-app ambition

Yesim has spent 2025 and 2026 building itself into more than a data shop, an industry trend we chart in our eSIM super-apps guide. Three extras stand out.

Virtual numbers. For $3.60 per month and up, billed monthly or yearly, you can rent a number from the UK, US, Netherlands or Canada to receive SMS in the app, aimed squarely at sign-ups, one-time passcodes and keeping WhatsApp or Telegram registered. The provider states the country list is expanding. These are receive-focused numbers rather than a full calling service, and Yesim's travel eSIMs themselves remain data-only.

Free VPN. Every plan includes a VPN at no extra cost, but only on iOS. Android users get nothing, and testers report the VPN cuts speeds by roughly a quarter to a third with no server selection. Useful for hotel wifi; not a replacement for a dedicated service.

Ycoins. The loyalty scheme returns 5% of purchases as app credit, with referral bonuses of 5 euros each way. Remember that Ycoins also feature in the refund policy, below, in a way you may like less.

Buying, using and topping up

Activation is by QR code or direct in-app installation, and reviewers consistently rate the app as one of Yesim's strengths: clean, multilingual and quick, with eSIMs live within minutes. The app holds about 4.6 stars on the App Store and 4.4+ on Google Play as of 19 July 2026. 5G is supported where partner networks allow it, with automatic network switching; testing found 5G easy to reach in Western Europe and the US, and one Swiss test recorded about 40 Mbps in the mountains on Salt and Sunrise. Performance elsewhere is patchier, with independent speed tests averaging 18-35 Mbps across tested Asian and Latin American markets, noticeably behind the fastest rivals. Our eSIM reliability guide explains how to judge network quality before you buy.

Hotspot use is allowed on all plans at no extra charge, a simpler stance than Holafly's 1GB/day tethering cap on single-trip plans. Top-ups are straightforward because the model is built around one reusable profile: add a package or wallet credit and keep going, with no new eSIM installation. Data plans and top-ups fall under the same 30-day refund window if genuinely unused.

Refunds, support and what customers complain about

The refund policy is stricter than the marketing suggests. Refunds apply only to unused, unexpired plans and top-ups within 30 days of purchase; an Unlim Day Pass counts as used the moment the device first connects. There is no general money-back guarantee, although confirmed technical malfunctions of the app are treated as an exception when reported promptly through the app or by email. Requests can take up to 15 business days to review, and any refund under 10 euros is paid in Ycoins credit rather than back to your card. That last clause matters because many single-country eSIM purchases cost less than 10 euros. See how this stacks up against the industry in our eSIM refund policies comparison.

Support is 24/7 via in-app chat and email, with a claimed average response of about six minutes. Customer reports suggest treating that number sceptically: one tester waited more than an hour in live chat, and another 2026 review measured typical responses at six to eight hours. Trustpilot sentiment sits around 4.3 from roughly 1,900 reviews, a decent score on a small base, though one industry publication notes that Trustpilot has flagged the profile and removed fake reviews, which warrants some caution when reading the headline figure. Recurring complaint themes across app stores and review platforms are familiar for the category: eSIMs failing to find a signal in specific countries, Android reinstallation problems, slow escalation when issues are technical, and frustration at refunds paid as credit. On the positive side, several reports confirm support did issue refunds when connections genuinely failed.

Advantages and disadvantages

Advantages

  • Pay & Fly wallet with 12-month validity is genuinely useful for frequent short trips.
  • One reusable eSIM profile; no reinstallation between trips or top-ups.
  • Hotspot allowed on every plan at no extra cost.
  • Polished, multilingual app rated 4.4-4.6 across both stores.
  • Extras no rival bundles the same way: virtual numbers from $3.60/month, free iOS VPN, 5% Ycoins cashback.
  • Solid European network performance, with 5G where available.

Disadvantages

  • Unlimited plans do not publish their high-speed allowance; throttling is unpredictable.
  • Mid-range prices; regularly undercut per gigabyte by Airalo, Nomad, MobiMatter and Saily.
  • Refunds under 10 euros paid only in app credit; strict 30-day, unused-only policy.
  • Support response times reported far slower than the advertised six minutes.
  • Data-only; no native calls or SMS, and the VPN is iOS-only.
  • Some destinations lack small 1-2GB plans, forcing light users into bigger bundles.

Who should choose what

  • Frequent multi-country travellers on short trips: Yesim's Pay & Fly wallet and reusable profile are the best fit in this review; load credit once and forget about plan shopping for a year.
  • One-destination holidaymakers who want cheap data: buy a fixed package from Airalo, Nomad or a MobiMatter deal instead; the maths in our best budget eSIM guide applies.
  • Heavy streamers who need dependable unlimited data: Holafly's track record is stronger and its trip lengths price flexibly; see Holafly vs Airalo for the daily-cap arithmetic.
  • Travellers who need to receive SMS abroad without roaming: Yesim's virtual numbers are a tidy in-app answer, provided a UK, US, Dutch or Canadian number suits your services.
  • Anyone who values published fair-use terms: choose a provider that states its thresholds; Airalo's 3GB/day rule is the industry's clearest, as our Airalo review details.

The verdict

Yesim is the strongest flexibility play among the mid-sized providers. The Pay & Fly wallet, one reusable eSIM, unrestricted hotspot and the virtual-number sideline make it closer to a travel connectivity toolkit than a simple data shop, and the app quality backs that up. For a frequent flyer hopping between European cities, it earns a genuine recommendation.

It is not the value pick for a single trip, and its transparency record needs work. Unpublished unlimited thresholds, refunds under 10 euros paid in credit and support that customer reports place well behind its own claims are all avoidable weaknesses. Price-driven buyers and unlimited-data holidaymakers will do better elsewhere. As a second eSIM kept installed for whenever travel happens, though, Yesim justifies its place on the shortlist.

Frequently asked questions

How much does Yesim cost?

Mid-range for the category. Independent 2026 checks found about $4.50 for 1GB/7 days in popular European destinations, $12-$24 for 5-20GB monthly bundles in tested markets, 7-day unlimited passes from about $20.40, and Pay & Fly pay-as-you-go from roughly $1 per GB in the cheapest countries.

Does Yesim have truly unlimited data?

Unlimited plans exist for many destinations, but Yesim does not clearly publish the daily high-speed allowance before throttling, which it attributes to local carrier fair-use policies. Expect a slowdown after heavy use, at an unpredictable point.

Can I use Yesim as a hotspot?

Yes. Tethering is permitted on all Yesim plans, including unlimited passes, at no extra cost and with no published device limit.

Does Yesim include a phone number?

Travel eSIMs are data-only. Yesim separately rents virtual numbers for the UK, US, Netherlands and Canada from $3.60 per month, designed for receiving SMS such as verification codes in the app.

Is Yesim legitimate and safe?

Yesim is a registered Swiss business (Genesis Group AG, Baar), founded in 2019, claiming over four million users, with a Trustpilot score around 4.3 and app ratings of 4.4-4.6. It is a real, functioning service; the main cautions are the strict refund terms and undisclosed unlimited thresholds covered in this review.
