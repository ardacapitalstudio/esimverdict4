Holafly vs MobiMatter: Which eSIM Is Better in 2026?

Holafly's unlimited-data eSIMs vs MobiMatter's 30-provider marketplace: we compare prices, refunds, hotspot rules, apps and support to find the better buy.

By the eSIM Verdict research desk Last checked 18 July 2026 14 min read

Some links on eSIMVerdict are affiliate links. If you buy through them we may earn a commission at no extra cost to you. Commercial relationships never affect rankings or conclusions; our assessments are based on independent research.

Holafly and MobiMatter sit at opposite ends of the travel eSIM market. Holafly sells its own product: unlimited-data plans for around 160 destinations, one predictable daily price, one support team and one refund policy. MobiMatter sells almost everything except its own product: it is a marketplace listing plans from more than 30 providers, including Airalo, Nomad, Ubigi, Sparks and eSIMGo, across 190+ countries, with prices starting at about $3.49.

The short answer: MobiMatter is dramatically cheaper for typical data use, often by a factor of three to five, and it is the better choice for price-conscious travellers who are comfortable comparing plans and reading the fine print. Holafly is the better choice when you want zero decisions and zero data anxiety: every plan is unlimited, support is a single 24/7 chat, and its refund terms are far more generous than the provider-dependent patchwork you get through a marketplace.

The deciding factor is not just price but accountability. When a Holafly eSIM fails, one company owns the problem. When a MobiMatter plan fails, resolution can depend on the underlying operator's policies, and MobiMatter's own terms state that it "does not in any way guarantee that any refund will be possible". This guide breaks down where each model wins, with verified July 2026 prices.

At a glance

  • Holafly: unlimited data in around 160 destinations, from $11.90 for 3 days. Hotspot capped at 1 GB per day. 24/7 chat support, single refund policy (up to 6 months for unused eSIMs). Data-only travel plans; a monthly subscription tier adds a US/UK/Canada phone number.
  • MobiMatter: marketplace of 30+ providers covering 190+ countries. Fixed-data plans from $3.49, frequently under $1 per GB. Some plans include calls and SMS. Top-ups and hotspot depend on the plan; refunds depend partly on the underlying provider.
  • Cheapest for most trips: MobiMatter, usually by a wide margin.
  • Simplest and safest if something goes wrong: Holafly.
  • Ratings: Holafly holds 4.7/5 on Trustpilot and about 4.8/5 in both app stores; MobiMatter holds 4.3/5 on Trustpilot (47,000+ reviews), 4.8/5 on iOS and 4.7/5 on Google Play.

Two opposite business models

Holafly is a vertically integrated brand. It negotiates directly with local carriers, sells only its own plans and answers for everything from activation to refunds. In December 2025 it reported passing $500 million in revenue and 10 million eSIMs sold, and it has been repositioning itself from a holiday product into something closer to an international operator, complete with monthly subscriptions.

MobiMatter is a comparison-and-checkout layer. It aggregates plans from more than 30 providers and lets the market compete on price inside its own storefront. The same Airalo or Nomad plan can appear cheaper on MobiMatter than on the provider's own site, and operator brands such as Sparks, eSIMGo, RoamVault and NextLink compete for each destination. A rewards scheme adds $5 referral credits and cashback on purchases.

The practical consequence: MobiMatter gives you choice and price competition, Holafly gives you consistency. Neither approach is inherently better, but they fail in different ways, and that matters most when something goes wrong.

How the marketplace model affects support and refunds

This is the sharpest difference between the two services, and it is written into the small print.

Holafly runs a single refund policy. You can claim a full refund within 6 months of purchase if the eSIM has not been activated or used, whether because your plans changed or your device turned out to be incompatible before scanning the QR code. If a connection failure is caused by Holafly or the destination network, a full or partial refund is available, minus a $3.50 administrative fee in some cases, with money returned within 5 to 10 business days. One company, one policy, one 24/7 chat.

MobiMatter's policy is tighter and partly outsourced. Its published terms allow a full or partial refund only when the service fails "due to genuine product malfunction" and support cannot fix it within 24 hours of you reporting it through the troubleshooting tool. Activated digital products are otherwise not eligible for cancellation or return, and the terms state that specific scenarios are governed by "the policies of the operator whose product has been sold", with MobiMatter mediating but not guaranteeing any refund. Processing can take 10 to 45 days.

Independent reviews echo this split. A January 2026 assessment by Saily found MobiMatter's refund outcomes "depend entirely on the provider" and reported user complaints about slow responses, particularly at weekends, while MobiMatter's own Trustpilot profile (4.3/5 from over 47,000 reviews) mixes praise for pricing with recurring frustration about escalations. Holafly's support consistently rates among the fastest in the industry, with reviewers reporting chat replies within minutes and a 4.7/5 Trustpilot score. The pattern is stark. A traveller using a moderate 1 GB per day would pay roughly four times more with Holafly in each of these markets. MobiMatter also lists long-validity plans with no Holafly equivalent, such as a Global 13 GB/365-day plan at $41.99. Holafly's pricing only becomes competitive if you genuinely use large amounts of data: at 4-5 GB per day, unlimited for $3.40 a day undercuts buying 60 GB or more of marketplace data. One caveat on headline prices: the cheapest plan is not always the best value per usable gigabyte. A $5.99 3 GB plan that runs dry on day four can end up costing more, in top-ups and hassle, than a mid-sized plan bought once.

Unlimited data: fair use vs speed caps

Both services sell "unlimited" plans, and neither means unrestricted high-speed data. The mechanics differ.

Holafly applies carrier fair-use policies. Its FAQ confirms that "some operators may reduce speeds after reaching a high-speed limit", with normal speeds returning the next day. Holafly does not publish a universal threshold, which is our main transparency criticism; independent 2026 analyses put typical limits at roughly 2-3 GB per day on affected networks, with throttled speeds between 256 Kbps and 1 Mbps. In practice most users never notice, but heavy streamers might.

MobiMatter's unlimited listings are openly speed-capped, because the underlying operators publish the rules. A representative example: eSIMGo's Dubai Unlimited 7-day plan at $25.99 gives 1 GB per day at full speed, "then speed will be limited to 512 Kbps for the remainder of the day". That is arguably more honest than an unpublished fair-use policy, but the high-speed allowance is smaller than Holafly's typical threshold, and 512 Kbps is barely usable for more than messaging.

Verdict on unlimited: Holafly's version is closer to genuinely unlimited; MobiMatter's unlimited plans are best treated as daily 1 GB plans with a slow fallback.

Hotspot, top-ups, calls and coverage

Hotspot. Holafly now allows tethering on its standard country and regional plans, capped at 1 GB of shared data per day, an improvement on the 500 MB limit reviewers reported in earlier years. That covers maps and email on a companion's phone, not a day of laptop work. On MobiMatter, hotspot rules are set per plan by each provider: many plans, including the eSIMGo unlimited series, enable it without a separate cap beyond the plan's own limits, while others block it. Check the spec sheet before buying.

Top-ups. Holafly's single-trip plans have no data top-ups because data is unlimited; you can extend the duration before expiry in most countries, though extensions are blocked in a few markets, such as Turkey, for regulatory reasons. MobiMatter supports top-ups on selected plans only, without reinstalling the eSIM, and availability depends on the provider.

Calls and SMS. Holafly's travel eSIMs are strictly data-only. Its Holafly Plans subscription changes that: the Unlimited tier includes a US, UK or Canada phone number. MobiMatter, unusually, lists three package types: data-only, number-without-service, and full-service plans with minutes and SMS included, flagged with a phone icon. Travellers who need a working local number have more options on MobiMatter today.

Coverage. MobiMatter's 190+ countries edge out Holafly's roughly 160 destinations, and the marketplace often carries several networks per country. Holafly counters with named tier-one partners, such as AT&T and T-Mobile in the USA and KDDI/SoftBank in Japan, so you know which network you are getting. On MobiMatter, provider coverage does not guarantee local network quality, and some cheaper plans route traffic through distant gateways, which can add latency. Remember that either option will almost certainly beat legacy operator charges; see eSIM vs roaming.

What changed in 2025-26

Holafly has moved fastest. In December 2025 it launched Holafly Plans, a monthly global subscription (Light 25 GB and Unlimited tiers, the latter around $59.95-64.90 per month at current pricing) covering 160+ destinations, with the Unlimited tier adding a phone number, a first for a major travel eSIM brand. Every plan now also includes "Always On", a free 1 GB of backup data that renews every 30 days, and the hotspot allowance on standard plans has doubled to 1 GB per day. Its pricing structure has also shifted from per-day purchases to fixed duration tiers starting at 3 days.

MobiMatter has changed less: reviews updated through June 2026 describe the same marketplace model, now spanning roughly 25-30 active providers and over 1,000 listed plans, with steady app improvements (iOS version 10.30.0 shipped on 30 June 2026) and an expanded cashback and referral scheme.

Advantages and disadvantages

Holafly

Advantages: truly simple, one unlimited plan per destination; strong named networks; 24/7 chat with fast replies; generous 6-month refund window for unused eSIMs; 1 GB/day hotspot; Always On backup data; monthly subscription with a phone number for long-stay travellers.

Disadvantages: expensive for light and moderate users; fair-use thresholds not published per plan; data-only on standard travel plans; smaller destination list; no data top-ups on single-trip plans.

MobiMatter

Advantages: consistently among the cheapest ways to buy travel data; enormous choice across 30+ providers, including plans with calls and SMS; 190+ countries; long-validity and yearly plans; transparent per-plan spec sheets; cashback and referral rewards; highly rated apps.

Disadvantages: refunds partly governed by the underlying operator, with no guarantee; support quality varies and escalations can be slow; "unlimited" plans are speed-capped, typically 1 GB/day then 512 Kbps-1 Mbps; quality varies between providers, so research is on you.

Who should choose what

The simplicity-seeker: Holafly. If you want to buy once, land, switch on and never think about data again, Holafly is built for you. Unlimited data removes rationing, support is one chat away, and a single refund policy protects the purchase. You pay a real premium for that peace of mind.

The bargain-hunter: MobiMatter. If you know roughly how much data you use and are happy spending ten minutes comparing plans, MobiMatter will usually cost a third or less of Holafly's price for the same trip. Stick to well-reviewed providers within the marketplace, screenshot the plan terms, and check hotspot and top-up rules before checkout.

The long-stay traveller: it depends on data appetite. For multi-month trips on moderate data, MobiMatter's large and yearly plans, such as global 365-day options, are excellent value. For heavy users who work abroad, Holafly Plans' unlimited monthly subscription with Always On backup and a phone number is the more complete product, at around $60 a month. Multi-country itineraries should also read our guide to the best eSIMs for multiple destinations.

The verdict

MobiMatter wins on price, coverage breadth and flexibility; Holafly wins on simplicity, support and refund protection. For most short trips where you can estimate your usage, MobiMatter is the better-value purchase, provided you accept that after-sales service depends partly on which of its 30+ providers you picked. If you would rather pay more and never worry about data caps, provider small print or arguing a refund through an intermediary, Holafly is worth its premium, and its 2025-26 updates (1 GB/day hotspot, Always On backup data, monthly plans with a phone number) have widened that comfort gap. Choose the marketplace for your wallet, choose the brand for your peace of mind. For how Holafly stacks up against a direct budget rival, see Holafly vs Airalo and our full Airalo review.

Frequently asked questions

Is MobiMatter legitimate?

Yes. It is an established marketplace with a 4.3/5 Trustpilot rating from more than 47,000 reviews, over 1 million Android app downloads and strong app-store scores. The main caution is that plan quality and refund handling vary by the underlying provider.

Is Holafly really unlimited?

Data volume is unlimited, but some partner carriers apply fair-use speed reductions after heavy daily use, with normal speeds returning the next day. Independent 2026 reviews estimate thresholds around 2-3 GB per day on affected networks. Hotspot sharing is separately capped at 1 GB per day.

Which is cheaper for a two-week trip to Europe?

MobiMatter, comfortably. A Sparks 10 GB/30-day Europe plan cost $10.49 at the time of checking, against $50.50 for Holafly's 15-day unlimited Europe eSIM. Holafly only wins on cost if you would otherwise need very large data volumes.

Can I make calls with either service?

Holafly's standard travel eSIMs are data-only, though its monthly Unlimited subscription includes a US, UK or Canada number. MobiMatter lists some full-service plans with a phone number, minutes and SMS, marked with a phone icon on the plan card.

What happens if my eSIM does not work?

Holafly's 24/7 chat handles issues directly, and unused eSIMs qualify for refunds up to 6 months after purchase. MobiMatter asks you to run its troubleshooting tool first; refunds are only offered for genuine malfunctions unresolved within 24 hours, and outcomes can depend on the underlying operator's policies.
