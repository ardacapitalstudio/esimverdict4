Saily eSIM Review 2026: How Good Is Nord Security's Travel eSIM?

Our independent Saily eSIM review for 2026: real prices, unlimited fair-use limits, built-in security tools, hotspot rules and how it compares with Airalo.

By the eSIM Verdict research desk Last checked 19 July 2026 12 min read

Saily is the travel eSIM from Nord Security, the Lithuanian company behind NordVPN, and that parentage shapes almost everything about it. The app bundles an ad blocker, web protection and a virtual location tool at no extra cost, the marketing leans hard on trust and privacy, and the product itself is unusually polished for a category full of thin storefronts. The ownership is worth knowing about for another reason too: it means your travel data and your security software could come from the same corporate group, which will appeal to some readers and give others pause.

On the numbers, Saily is competitive rather than cheap. Plans start at US$3.99 for 1GB in big destinations such as the USA and Turkey, unlimited plans carry a clearly published daily fast-data allowance, and hotspot use is explicitly unrestricted - a genuine rarity. Coverage spans roughly 190 to 200+ destinations depending on whose count you read, which puts it in the same bracket as Airalo and ahead of most challengers.

The catches are real but manageable: everything is data-only unless you pay extra for Saily's new US phone number, the security tools are much less than a VPN despite the branding, and the refund small print is vaguer than we would like. Here is what our research found, fact by fact.

At a glance

  • Coverage: around 200 destinations; regional plans (Europe covers 35 countries) and a global plan covering 113-121 countries
  • Prices: from US$3.99/1GB (USA, Turkey); UK from US$4.49/1GB; Europe regional from US$4.99/1GB
  • Unlimited plans: yes, with a published fair-use cap - 5GB/day at full speed in the USA, 3GB/day on the Europe plan, then up to 1 Mbps
  • Hotspot: allowed, "no restrictions" per Saily's plan pages
  • Calls and SMS: data-only by default; optional US (+1) number from US$0.99/month
  • Security extras: ad blocker, web protection, virtual location (115+ locations) included free; NOT a VPN
  • Support: 24/7 live chat and email
  • Trustpilot: 4.7/5 displayed on Saily's site, July 2026

Who is behind Saily, and why it matters

Saily is a young product by eSIM standards, promoted as being "by the creators of NordVPN". That heritage buys it instant credibility in a market where anyone can white-label an eSIM reseller platform, and it shows in the product: the app is slick, documentation is thorough, and support runs 24/7.

It also raises a question most eSIM reviews skip. An eSIM provider routes your mobile traffic, so it can see which domains you visit even when the content is encrypted; a security company that also sells a VPN now sits at both ends of that equation. Nord's privacy reputation is strong, but if you want to understand exactly what any eSIM provider can observe, our guide to what your eSIM provider can see covers it in detail.

Coverage and plan structure

Saily sells three plan types: local single-country eSIMs, seven regional plans, and a global plan. Saily's own site talks of coverage in "hundreds of countries"; independent reviews count between 190 and 200+ destinations, with the global plan spanning 121 countries by cybernews' 2026 count. In practice that matches Airalo's 200+ claim closely enough that coverage will rarely decide between them.

Fixed-data plans run 30 days (1GB plans 7 days); unlimited plans let you pick a duration between 5 and 30 days at checkout. Every purchase earns 3% back in Saily credits. One structural advantage over Airalo: you install a single Saily eSIM once and buy plans for any destination on it, rather than juggling separate eSIM profiles per country.

The Europe plan's 35-country list includes the UK, Switzerland and Turkey but skips the Western Balkans, a gap worth checking against your itinerary - our best eSIM for multiple destinations guide maps who covers what. Note also Saily's own warning that access to the Saily app may be limited inside Turkey, so set up before you fly.

Unlimited plans and the fair-use small print

Saily is more transparent about "unlimited" than most. The plan pages state the daily full-speed allowance next to the price: 5GB per day in the USA, 3GB per day on the Europe regional plan. Beyond that, speeds drop to "up to 1 Mbps" until the 24-hour period resets - enough for maps and messaging, marginal for video.

The fair-usage policy adds a broader catch-all: usage "greater than the average usage of 99% of other unlimited plan users" can be treated as excessive, and the prohibited-conduct list includes "excessive tethering/hotspots". For an ordinary traveller neither clause should ever bite, but a family streaming through one hotspot all fortnight is exactly the profile that could. Compare Holafly, which publishes no fast-data threshold at all, and Holiday.com's 3GB/day followed by a far more usable 10 Mbps throttle. On throttle behaviour Holiday.com wins; on price per day for short trips, Saily's US$48.99 for up to 30 days can undercut Holafly's US$6.90/day dramatically on longer stays.

The security features: useful, but not a VPN

Three tools ship free in the Saily app, toggled from the eSIM settings screen.

  • Ad blocker. Works by DNS filtering, so it strips most third-party ads network-wide and saves data, but ads served from a site's own domain still get through.
  • Web protection. Blocks known malicious domains and limits trackers - essentially a DNS blocklist, similar to NordVPN's Threat Protection Lite.
  • Virtual location. Lets you route traffic to appear in one of 115+ locations, useful for reaching home services abroad.

Be clear about what this is not. There is no full-device encrypted tunnel, no kill switch and no VPN protocol here; cybernews describes the virtual location tool as location masking rather than a VPN substitute, and Saily itself sells NordVPN separately. If a proper VPN is the point, our best eSIM for VPN users guide covers the pairings that work - and note the irony that Holiday.com, built by the ExpressVPN team, bundles no VPN in its eSIM either.

Calls, texts and the US$0.99 phone number

Standard Saily plans are data-only: no number, no SMS, with WhatsApp, FaceTime and other VoIP apps as the workaround. New for 2025-26, Saily now sells an optional US (+1) phone number from US$0.99 a month with calls, texts and - crucially - the ability to receive verification codes, with minutes sold as add-on packs. For anyone whose bank insists on texting a code, that is a genuinely useful extra almost no travel eSIM rival offers. If you need a permanent second number rather than a travel bolt-on, see our guide to running an eSIM as a permanent second line.

Saily Ultra and other 2025-26 changes

Saily has shipped quickly over the past year. Alongside the phone number, it introduced Saily Ultra, a monthly subscription aimed at frequent travellers: unlimited global data across 113 countries, priority support, 8% cashback in credits and access to the Nord Security suite - NordVPN, NordPass, NordLocker and Incogni - in one renewing plan. Cybernews lists Ultra tiers from US$26.99 to US$53.99 a month. Ultra is the one place a real VPN does come bundled; standard pay-as-you-go plans do not renew by themselves and are topped up manually in the app. 5G now features across supported destinations, with 3G/4G/LTE fallback depending on local partner networks.

Hotspot, activation and everyday use

Saily's plan pages state hotspot use is "unlimited access with no restrictions", and its Europe marketing invites you to "share as much of your mobile hotspot... as you want" - the most generous published tethering stance of any major provider, subject only to the unlimited-plan fair-use clause above. Plans activate automatically when you land and connect, you get a 30-day window to activate after purchase, and the app warns you at 80% data usage. Top-ups are bought in-app against the same installed eSIM.

Reputation, support and refunds

Saily displays a 4.7/5 Trustpilot score on its site (July 2026), from a review base cybernews puts in the five figures, alongside 35,000+ App Store and 61,000+ Google Play reviews. Earth SIMs' 2026 review rated support 4.0/5, reporting live-chat response times of 15 to 45 minutes across eight interactions - respectable, though short of Holafly's near-instant chat.

Refunds are Saily's least transparent area. Independent reviews report a money-back window for plans that have not been activated, but disagree on its length - Earth SIMs states 14 days, Freetring 30 days - and Saily buries the details in its terms rather than publishing a simple policy page. Once a plan is activated, expect a refund only for verified technical faults, in line with the industry norm our eSIM refund policies comparison documents. Check the current terms at purchase; this is the one area where Saily trails the clarity of its own pricing pages.

Advantages and disadvantages

Advantages

  • Transparent unlimited plans: daily fast-data caps printed next to the price
  • Unrestricted hotspot policy, unique among the big names
  • One eSIM profile serves every destination; 3% credit back on purchases
  • Free ad blocker, web protection and 115+ virtual locations
  • Optional US number (US$0.99/month) receives verification texts
  • 24/7 support and a polished, beginner-friendly app

Disadvantages

  • Data-only unless you add the phone number; no local numbers outside the US
  • Security tools are DNS filtering and location masking, not a VPN
  • Refund window poorly documented and inconsistently reported
  • Mid-pack per-GB pricing; Turkey app-access caveat
  • Europe plan skips the Western Balkans; 1 Mbps throttle after unlimited caps

Who should choose what

  • Choose Saily if you tether other devices, want unlimited data with honest small print, need a number for verification texts, or simply value a big-brand owner with 24/7 support.
  • Choose Airalo if you want the widest headline coverage, more regional plan variety and frequent promotional pricing - our Airalo review has the full picture.
  • Choose Holiday.com if unlimited data with a gentle 10 Mbps throttle matters more than extras.
  • Look elsewhere if you need the absolute cheapest gigabyte - marketplace models win on raw price, as our best budget eSIM ranking shows - or if you need real calls and SMS on a local number.

The verdict

Judged on our standard criteria - price, coverage, network quality, plan flexibility, transparency, ease of use, hotspot support and customer service - Saily lands firmly in the top tier of travel eSIMs in 2026. It is the category's transparency leader on unlimited data and its hotspot policy is the most traveller-friendly we have found, while pricing sits mid-pack and the security branding promises slightly more than DNS filtering delivers. Against Airalo it trades a little coverage breadth for a better app model and honest fair-use disclosure; against Holiday.com it trades throttle speed for broader coverage and its bundled extras. If Saily published a clear refund policy it would be pushing for the overall crown; as it stands, it is an easy recommendation for tetherers, families sharing data and anyone who values knowing exactly what "unlimited" means before paying.

Frequently asked questions

Is Saily owned by NordVPN?

Saily is built by Nord Security, the company behind NordVPN, NordPass and NordLocker. It is marketed as "by the creators of NordVPN", and the Saily Ultra subscription bundles the full Nord app suite, though standard Saily plans do not include any VPN.

Does Saily really have unlimited data?

Yes, with a published fair-use cap. Unlimited plans include a daily full-speed allowance - 5GB per day in the USA, 3GB per day on the Europe plan - after which speeds drop to around 1 Mbps until the next 24-hour period. Saily also reserves the right to curb usage far above that of 99% of unlimited users.

Can I use Saily as a hotspot?

Yes. Saily's plan pages state hotspot access is unlimited with no restrictions on standard plans, which makes it one of the most tethering-friendly major providers. The only caveat is a fair-use clause against "excessive tethering" on unlimited plans.

Does Saily include a VPN?

No. Standard plans include an ad blocker, web protection and a virtual location tool, which work by DNS filtering and location masking rather than an encrypted VPN tunnel. A full VPN (NordVPN) is only bundled with the separate Saily Ultra subscription.

Can I make calls and send texts with Saily?

By default Saily is data-only, so calls and texts run over apps such as WhatsApp. Since 2025-26 you can add an optional US (+1) phone number from US$0.99 a month, which supports calls, SMS and receiving verification codes, with minutes sold as separate packs.
