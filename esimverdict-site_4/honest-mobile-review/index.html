Honest Mobile Review 2026: The B Corp UK Network With a Clever Backup eSIM

Our independent Honest Mobile review for 2026 covers Classic SIM prices on Three, the £3.75/month Smart SIM backup eSIM, roaming rules and who should buy.

By the eSIM Verdict research desk Last checked 19 July 2026 12 min read

Honest Mobile is a small, app-first UK mobile network with two products that do very different jobs. Its Classic SIM is a conventional monthly plan running on Three's network, from £12.15 per month with unlimited UK calls and texts and a loyalty discount that trims your bill every month you stay. Its Smart SIM, launched in March 2025, is the more interesting one for our readers: a £10 per month (or £45 per year) data-only eSIM that connects to EE, O2, Three and Vodafone in the UK and roams in 180+ countries at no extra cost, with unlimited data restricted to a whitelist of 500+ essential apps.

Let us be clear about what Honest Mobile is not. It is not a travel eSIM in the Airalo or Holafly mould, and it makes no sense to buy it for a single fortnight abroad. It is a UK domestic MVNO, and a certified B Corp that markets itself as a carbon-negative network. Where it earns a place in a traveller's line-up is as a permanent fixture: a cheap always-on second line, a UK number you keep while living abroad on the Classic SIM, or a multi-network safety net that catches you when your main SIM has no signal or no data left.

Our research found a company with an excellent Trustpilot reputation, genuinely original products, and two honest caveats: the Classic SIM is pricey for its allowances, and the companion app that the whole service depends on has a rough patch of Android reviews. Here is the full picture.

At a glance

  • What it is: UK MVNO and certified B Corp; app-managed eSIM or physical SIM
  • Classic SIM: Three's network, 5G included, from £12.15/month, unlimited calls and texts
  • Smart SIM: backup/second eSIM, £10/month or £45/year, EE + O2 + Three + Vodafone, 180+ countries roaming, unlimited data on 500+ approved apps only
  • EU roaming (Classic): 5 free roaming days per trip, then £2/day, up to 20GB
  • Loyalty discount: bill falls every month, up to 30% off
  • Trustpilot: 4.7/5 from over 3,500 reviews
  • Weak spots: high per-GB pricing, app login complaints on Android, Smart SIM blocks browsing and streaming

The Classic SIM: a fair but expensive Three plan

Honest's core product piggybacks on Three's masts, which Honest says reaches 99.8% of the UK population with 5G included as standard. Three's network is mid-merger: since Vodafone and Three combined in June 2025, coverage and speeds on Three hosts have been improving measurably, which strengthens Honest's hand in rural areas. You get unlimited UK calls and texts on every tier, unrestricted hotspot use, Wi-Fi calling, and a choice of eSIM or physical SIM.

The signature feature is the loyalty discount. Instead of the industry's usual mid-contract price rises, Honest cuts your bill slightly every month you stay, compounding to a maximum of 30% off. The unlimited plan therefore drifts from £25.00 down towards £17.50 over time, and the 4GB plan from £12.15 towards £9.45. It is a refreshing inversion of telecoms pricing psychology, and it rewards exactly the long-tenure customer this product is designed for.

The catch is the starting price. £12.15 for 4GB is a lot in a market where budget MVNOs on the same Three network sell far larger allowances for less, and there are only three tiers to choose from. You are paying a premium for the B Corp positioning, the app experience and the discount mechanic, not for cheap gigabytes. If cheap gigabytes are the goal, our best budget eSIM guide is the better starting point.

Roaming on the Classic SIM

Every Classic plan includes 5 free EU roaming days per trip, with roaming data capped at 20GB; after the fifth day you pay £2 per day, and fair use limits roaming to around 60 days per year. That structure suits short hops to Europe well and month-long stays poorly. Outside Europe, our research found Honest's answer is not a published day-pass table but its own Smart SIM, which it positions as the travel companion for the rest of the world. The Smart SIM: the backup eSIM idea, properly executed

The Smart SIM is why Honest Mobile appears on a travel eSIM site at all. Launched in March 2025 and eSIM-only, it is a data-only second line that sits alongside your main SIM in any dual-SIM phone. In the UK it can connect to EE, O2, Three or Vodafone, switching to whichever has the strongest signal; abroad it roams in more than 180 countries, including the US, Canada, Japan, Australia and Brazil, at no extra charge. You can flip to it manually in a few taps, or let it take over automatically when your main SIM loses service entirely.

The pricing is the striking part: £10 per month on a rolling basis, or £45 per year, which works out at £3.75 per month. For that you get unlimited data with no fair use threshold, but with a fundamental restriction: only apps on Honest's whitelist work. At launch the list covered 200+ apps; by mid-2026 it had grown past 500, including WhatsApp, Google Maps, Uber, Citymapper, Monzo, PayPal and Wise. Social media, video streaming, open web browsing and VPNs are blocked, and there is no phone number, so no calls, no SMS and, importantly, no 999 dialling on the Smart SIM itself.

Independent testing supports the concept. SIM Only Finder's eight-month hands-on review found the Smart SIM switched between the four UK networks as promised and kept maps, messaging, ride-hailing and banking apps working across Miami, the British Virgin Islands and the Dominican Republic, though the reviewer preferred switching manually because automatic failover was sometimes slow, and noted that maritime networks are not supported and data usage reporting is approximate.

Two design decisions deserve credit. First, the multi-network access genuinely covers all four UK spectrum holders, which no mainstream UK SIM does on its own. Second, the app whitelist is what makes unlimited roaming in 180+ countries affordable at £45 a year; wholesale roaming data is expensive, and by blocking video and browsing Honest keeps consumption to the megabytes that actually matter in an emergency. You are buying connectivity insurance, not a data plan, and it is priced like insurance.

Where Honest Mobile fits in a traveller's setup

Treat Honest as infrastructure, not a trip purchase. Three scenarios make sense.

A permanent second line. Modern phones hold multiple eSIMs, and our guide to running an eSIM as a permanent second line explains why a standing second profile beats buying ad hoc. The Smart SIM at £45 per year is one of the cheapest standing lines available in the UK, provided you accept that it is data-only and app-limited.

UK number retention and dual-SIM travel. Expats and long-term travellers who need to keep a UK number for banking OTPs can hold a Classic SIM (from £12.15 per month, ideally shrinking with loyalty discounts) as their UK anchor, then add a local travel eSIM for data. How to juggle the two lines is covered in our guide to running two eSIMs at once. Note that cheaper PAYG SIMs can do bare-bones number retention for less; Honest's version buys you a proper plan and app around it.

Backup coverage at home and abroad. For UK residents, the Smart SIM covers the not-spots and outages of any single network, including the one your main SIM is on. For visitors to Britain, it is not the right product: it has no UK phone number and blocks browsing, so a conventional travel eSIM from our best eSIM for the UK ranking remains the correct buy for a holiday.

Sustainability: the B Corp angle

Honest Mobile is a certified B Corporation, a member of 1% for the Planet, and describes itself as a carbon-negative network, funding afforestation, biochar and direct air capture projects and letting customers track the impact in the app. We treat sustainability claims as a tiebreaker rather than a ranking criterion, but Honest's certifications are real, third-party audited frameworks rather than self-declared greenwash, and no major UK network matches the B Corp badge.

Reputation: excellent reviews, awkward app

The Trustpilot picture is strong: 4.7 out of 5 from more than 3,500 reviews, with recurring praise for fast, human customer support through the app. The most common complaint theme in negative reviews is eSIM setup difficulty, particularly during transfers between phones.

The app itself is more mixed, which matters for a network with no phone support line and where everything from sign-up to network switching happens in-app. On Google Play the app holds a 2.9-star rating from 256 reviews (50,000+ downloads, updated 15 July 2026), with complaints centring on magic-link login loops and sign-up verification friction; Honest responds to reviews and says it has streamlined login, but the gripes persist into 2026. Our research suggests the service works smoothly once set up, and the reliability fundamentals of the underlying networks are sound, but a company this app-dependent needs its Android experience to be better than this.

Advantages and disadvantages

Advantages

  • Smart SIM is a unique product: four UK networks plus 180+ country roaming from £3.75/month
  • Loyalty discount cuts bills by up to 30% instead of raising prices mid-contract
  • Unlimited UK calls, texts and unrestricted hotspot on all Classic plans
  • 5 free EU roaming days per trip on Classic plans
  • Trustpilot 4.7/5 with consistently praised support
  • Certified B Corp, carbon negative, 1% for the Planet member
  • Flexible monthly terms and a 14-day money-back guarantee on the Smart SIM

Disadvantages

  • Classic SIM per-GB pricing is high against budget rivals on the same Three network
  • Smart SIM blocks social media, streaming, browsing and VPNs, and cannot call 999
  • No phone number on the Smart SIM; SMS-based bank codes will not arrive on it
  • Android app reviews are poor (2.9 stars), and the service is app-dependent
  • Only three Classic tiers, and EU roaming is day-limited with a 20GB cap
  • eSIM setup and transfer complaints appear regularly in reviews

Who should choose what

  • UK residents wanting signal insurance: Smart SIM at £45/year is the category's best value backup line
  • Frequent travellers who need maps, messaging and taxis abroad: Smart SIM covers the essentials in 180+ countries for less than most single-trip unlimited eSIMs
  • Expats keeping a UK number: Classic 4GB as an anchor line, paired with local travel eSIMs
  • Ethically-minded UK switchers: Classic SIM, if the B Corp premium is worth £3-5/month to you
  • Data-heavy users and streamers: look elsewhere; the Smart SIM blocks streaming and the Classic plans are poor per-GB value
  • Visitors to the UK: a conventional travel eSIM from our best eSIM for England guide, not this

The verdict

As a mainstream UK network, Honest Mobile is likeable but expensive: a well-reviewed, genuinely sustainable MVNO whose loyalty pricing is clever and whose allowances cost too much. As a traveller's tool, it is far more interesting. The Smart SIM is, on our research, the cheapest way in Britain to put all four UK networks and 180+ roaming destinations behind your main SIM, and at £45 per year it undercuts almost every alternative way of buying resilience. The app whitelist is a real constraint you must go in understanding: this is a lifeline for maps, messages, payments and rides, not a data plan. In the backup and second-line category, Honest Mobile is our current benchmark; as a primary SIM, it is a considered lifestyle choice rather than a value pick.

Frequently asked questions

What network does Honest Mobile use?

The Classic SIM runs on Three's network, with 5G included and coverage that Honest puts at 99.8% of the UK population. The Smart SIM is different: it is a multi-network eSIM that can connect to EE, O2, Three and Vodafone, using whichever signal is strongest where you are.

How does the Honest Mobile Smart SIM work as a backup?

It installs as a second, data-only eSIM alongside your main SIM in a dual-SIM phone. When your main SIM has no coverage or no data, you switch to the Smart SIM manually in a few taps, or it can take over automatically if your primary service fails. Data is unlimited but only works in 500+ approved apps such as WhatsApp, Google Maps, Uber and banking apps.

Does the Smart SIM really roam free in 180+ countries?

Yes, roaming in 180+ countries is included in the £10 monthly or £45 annual price with no extra roaming charges. The same app whitelist applies abroad, so browsing, streaming and social media will not work, and there is no phone number for calls or SMS. Independent testing found it working reliably in the US and the Caribbean.

Is Honest Mobile good value as a main SIM?

Only if its ethics and loyalty pricing matter to you. £12.15 for 4GB or £25 for unlimited is above the market rate for Three-hosted MVNOs, although the loyalty discount can eventually cut bills by up to 30%. Pure value hunters will do better elsewhere, particularly for large data allowances.

Can I use Honest Mobile instead of a travel eSIM?

Not as a straight swap. Classic SIM roaming is Europe-focused (5 free days per trip, then £2/day, 20GB cap), and the Smart SIM cannot browse the web or stream. The smart pattern is to keep Honest as a permanent backup or UK anchor line and buy a normal travel eSIM for full internet access on longer trips.
